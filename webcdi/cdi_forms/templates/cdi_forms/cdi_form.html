    In this section, you will be asked about the phrases and words that your child "understands" and "understands and says". Here are some examples.

    Your child "understands" a word or phrase:



    If s/he shows that s/he knows what the word/phrase means by responding to it appropriately. For example, when you say "Get the ball" s/he turns and tries to find the ball.

    DO check "understands".


    DON'T check "understands" if you don't think that s/he knows meaning of that word/phrase. S/he might have heard it before, like in a story, but you don't think s/he would know what it means.


    Your child "understands and says" a word or phrase:



    If s/he says the word/phrase when trying to describe an object or something that happened and you think s/he has a meaning for that word/phrase.

    DO check "understands and says".


    It's fine if your child can't say the whole word or says it his/her own way. It's also fine if your child says words that have the same meaning but are special to your own family (e.g., "nana" for "grandma").

    DO check "understands and says".


    DON’T check "understands and says" if your child is just stringing sounds together and you don't think it is a "real" word that means something.


    DON'T ask your child to repeat the words on the list. This doesn't count!

    DON'T check "understands and says".
